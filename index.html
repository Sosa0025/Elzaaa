History of Elza...

Elza is a neo-grotesque typeface inspired by Helvetica and Univers, emphasizing consistency and simplicity. It features digital technology that allows for smooth transitions between different weights and widths. The design includes small straight segments on round letters, giving it a clean, mechanical look.

As the largest family in Blackletra Type Foundry’s collection, Elza includes 58 fonts across three widths and two optical sizes. It offers oblique versions instead of true italics, creating clear style contrasts. Each font has around 900 glyphs, supporting multiple languages and OpenType features.

Elza also offers a variable version with adjustable weight, width, and slant. The typeface honors Brazilian singer Elza Soares while providing versatility for various design projects (In 1999, London BBC Radio elected Elza Soares as the Brazilian Singer of the Millennium).
